Have, Has – Usage

🎴 Have , Has இருக்கு

I , we , you , they → Have
He , she , it , name → Has

✔ I have a car.
✔ He has a car.
❌ He have a car.
❌ I have brother.
✔ I have a brother.

More examples:

  • We have relatives in Cochin.
  • They have a business in Dubai.
  • My father has 3 sisters.

“Have” vs “Have got”

Confusion 😇

Which one is correct?
I have 2 brothers.   vs   I have got 2 brothers.

✅ Both are correct

✔ (formal)I have a car.
✔ (less formal)I have got a car.
✔He has a car.
✔He has got a kid.

Correcting common mistakes:

❌I have fever.
✔I have a fever. (temperature)
❌I have headache.
✔I have a headache.
✔I have got a headache.
✔She has got a headache.

Sibling vocabulary (உடன்பிறப்புகள்):

  • Niece – பெண்
  • Nephew – ஆண்
  • Cousin

Negative Forms

Example sentences:

  • I have a car.
  • He has a car.

Which one is correct?

I don’t have a car.
I have no car.

In spoken English, we usually prefer:
✅ I don’t have a car.
❌/✔ I have no car. (less common in speech)

✔I don't have a car.
✔I haven't got a car.
✔I have no car. (more formal / written)

More negative examples:

  • He has a driving license.
  • ❌He have no driving licence.
  • ❌✔ He has no driving license. (ok in some contexts, less used in spoken)
  • He doesn't have a driving license. ✔
  • He hasn't got a driving license. ✔

Remember: He doesn’t have ❌ He doesn’t has

  • Fahad doesn't have a job.
  • They don't have a house.
  • We don't have any hope.
  • My father doesn't have any job.

Questions with “Do / Does”

🎴 Questions

  • Do you have brothers?
  • ❌✔You have brothers? (Spoken English)
  • ❌Have you brothers?
  • Have you got brothers? ✔

Using Does

  • He doesn't have a mobile phone. – அவனிடம் mobile phone இல்லை.
  • Does he have a mobile phone? – அவனிடம் mobile phone இருக்கிறதா?
  • ❌❌Has he mobile phone?
  • ✔❌He has a mobile phone? (Spoken)
  • Has he got a mobile phone?

Using Do

  • We have class tomorrow.
  • We don't have class tomorrow. – நமக்கு நாளை வகுப்பு இல்லை.
  • Do we have class today? ✔
  • ✔❌We have class today? (Spoken)

“How many brothers…?”

  • How many brothers do you have? ✔ (formal)
  • How many brothers have you got? ✔ (informal)
  • ✔❌How many brothers you have?
  • How many brothers does he have? ✔
  • How many brothers has he got? ✔
  • ✔❌How many brothers he has?

Home work

  1. அவனுக்கு வேலை இல்லை. அவனுக்கு எத்தனை குழந்தைகள் இருக்கிறார்கள்?
  2. அவர்களுக்கு நாளை ஒரு function இருக்கிறது.
  3. அவருக்கு 3 குழந்தைகள் இருக்கிறார்கள்.
  4. எங்களுக்கு நாளை வகுப்பு இருக்கிறதா?
  5. அவனுக்கு work experience இருக்கிறதா?
  6. அவனிடம் பணம் இல்லை.
  7. என் அப்பாவிற்கு 3 சகோதரிகள் உள்ளனர்.
  8. ரஜினிகாந்த்க்கு பெண் பிள்ளைகள் இருக்கிறதா?
  9. Chennai silks க்கு கோயம்புத்தூரில் கிளை இருக்கிறது.
  10. இதற்கு 3 வருடம் warranty இருக்கிறது.
Day 3 – Articles A · An · The
Countable / Uncountable · Rules & Exercises

🔊Day 3 – Article Lesson Audio

Articles:

  • 🛑 A
  • 🛑 An
  • 🛑 The

Articles are used with countable nouns.

Never use a or an with a word that is plural or uncountable.

❌ I have a cars.
✔ I have a car.

❌ He loves a milk.
✔ He loves milk.

“A” vs “An”

An – used before words with vowel sound.

A – used before words with consonant sound.

  • 🛑 an honest man
  • 🛑 an hour
  • 🌀 a university
  • 🌀 a user

A / An vs The

A / An – used when we don't speak about a specific thing.
The – used when we speak about a specific thing.

  • I caught a train.
    The train was late.
  • I was talking to a man.
    The man was laughing.
  • She gave him a present.
    The present was expensive.

“The” is used when it is obvious which thing we are speaking about:

  • I cleaned the bathroom this morning.
  • He travelled around the world.
  • The sun is hot today.

Articles – Exercise

  • Danny wanted _____ new bicycle for Christmas.
  • Jennifer tasted _____ birthday cake her mother had made.
  • The children have _____ new teacher called Mr. Green.
  • All pupils must obey _____ rules.
  • Dad turned on _____ radio to listen to _____ news.
  • Alex is in Boston studying for _____ MBA.
  • The teacher read _____ interesting article from the newspaper.
  • There was _____ huge crowd of people outside the church.
  • Julie talked for _____ hour about her school project.
  • _____ European expert was invited to speak to the committee.

Preposition of Time – IN (Parts of the day, Months, Seasons, Years)
பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
in + Parts of the day
in the morning
in the afternoon
in the evening
  • I have my classes in the evening.
    எனக்கு மாலையில் வகுப்புகள் உள்ளன.
  • I woke up early in the morning.
in + Months
in January
in February
  • I have my exams in January.
    எனக்கு ஜனவரியில் தேர்வுகள் உள்ளன.
  • They are getting married in May.
in + Seasons
in (the) spring
in (the) summer
  • We are planning a trip to Ooty in the summer.
    கோடையில் ஊட்டிக்கு சுற்றுலா செல்ல திட்டமிட்டுள்ளோம்.
  • The sun looks bigger in winter.
in + Years
in 1990
in 2020
  • I was born in 1990.
    நான் 1990 இல் பிறந்தேன்.
  • We moved to Chennai in 2002.
Preposition of Time – IN (Decades, Centuries, Weeks, Periods, Holidays)
பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
in + Decades
in the 1960s
in the seventies
  • Our house was built in the 1960's.
    எங்கள் வீடு 1960-களில் கட்டப்பட்டது.
  • My grandmother was born in the 1920's.
in + Centuries
in the 15th century
in the 21st century
  • We are living in the 21st Century.
    நாம் 21 ஆம் நூற்றாண்டில் வாழ்கிறோம்.
  • The palace was built in the 15th century.
in + Weeks
in a week
in 2 weeks
  • I am travelling to Dubai in a week.
    நான் ஒரு வாரத்தில் துபாய்க்கு பயணம் செல்கிறேன்.
  • My classes will start in 2 weeks.
in + Periods of time
in the past
in the next century
in the future
in the middle ages
in a moment
  • I will call you back in a moment.
    சிறிது நேரத்தில் உங்களை மீண்டும் அழைக்கிறேன்.
  • In the past online learning was not possible.
in + Holidays (காலத்தில்)
in the Easter holiday
in the summer vacation
  • Where did you go in the summer vacation?
    கோடைவிடுமுறையில் எங்கு சென்றீர்கள்?
  • We are planning to visit our grandparents in the Easter holidays.
Preposition of Time – ON
பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
on + Specific days
on my birthday
on my wedding day
on that day
  • It was raining heavily on that day.
    அன்று கனமழை பெய்து கொண்டிருந்தது.
  • My brother gifted this dress on my birthday.
on + Time
on the weekend (U.S.)
on weekdays
on time
  • We reached the venue on time.
    நாங்கள் சரியான நேரத்தில் இடத்தை அடைந்தோம்.
  • This shop is open only on weekends.
on + Day + Parts of day
on Sunday morning(s)
on Friday afternoon(s)
on Monday evening(s)
  • I wake up late on Sundays.
    ஞாயிற்றுக்கிழமைகளில் நான் தாமதமாக எழுவேன்.
  • We have a meeting on Monday evening.

பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
in + Countries
in India
in America
  • I live in India.
    நான் இந்தியாவில் வசிக்கிறேன்.

in + Cities
in Madurai
in Chennai
  • He lives in Chennai.
    அவர் சென்னையில் வசிக்கிறார்.
  • Our office is in Coimbatore.

in + Enclosed Space

Preposition of Time – AT
பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
at + Hours
at 7 am
at 12 o'clock
  • The class begins at 9 am.
    வகுப்பு காலை 9 மணிக்கு தொடங்குகிறது.
  • We will meet you today at 6 o'clock.
at + Parts of the day
at night
at noon / midday
at midnight
  • He came home very late at night.
    அவர் இரவு மிகவும் தாமதமாக வீட்டுக்கு வந்தார்.
  • I heard a sound at the midnight.
at + Time
at present
at the weekend
at breakfast
at the moment
  • I am busy at the moment.
    தற்போது நான் வேலைபாயாக இருக்கிறேன்.
  • At present I don't have any plans for travel.
Preposition of Place – AT
பயன்பாடுகள் (Usage) உதாரணங்கள் (Examples)
at + Exact Addresses or Intersections
at 23, Richie Street
at 4th cross street
  • My house is at 4th Cross Street.
    எனது வீடு 4வது குறுக்குத் தெருவில் உள்ளது.
  • He stays at Richie Street.
at + Specific Locations / Points
at Fun mall
at the corner
at the bus stop
  • There is a shop at the corner of this street.
    இந்தத் தெருவின் மூலையில் ஒரு கடை உள்ளது.
  • I will be waiting for you at the bus stop tomorrow.
Exceptions – Prepositions of Time
Rule / Exception Examples
Do NOT use in/on with:
yesterday, tomorrow, this, last, next
  • I have a meeting on tomorrow ❌
  • I have a meeting tomorrow ✔️
Do NOT use AT, IN, ON with:
every, this
  • I go home at every Easter ❌
  • I go home every Easter ✔️
  • We will call you in this evening ❌
  • We will call you this evening ✔️
Use “at night”
  • In the night ❌ or at night ✔️
  • I will call you at night ✔️

Note:
In the night usually refers to one particular night.
At night refers to any night in general.

Example: I was awake in the night playing video games.
